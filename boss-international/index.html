Boss International Studies is a study visa consultancy headquartered in Chandigarh, with branches across the region, serving students and families from Punjab and Haryana ‚Äî two of India's most active study-abroad corridors. Their clients have real dreams, real savings on the line, and very little tolerance for broken promises.

The market wasn't short on consultancies. Every billboard, every WhatsApp forward, every Facebook ad said essentially the same thing. The challenge wasn't awareness ‚Äî it was belief. Getting a family from Ambala or Ludhiana to trust a brand they discovered on Instagram required a fundamentally different approach than conventional marketing.

01

The feed was rebuilt around one idea: students don't trust brands, they trust people who look like them. Every content piece was designed to feel regional, real, and relatable ‚Äî not polished in a way that felt out of reach for a family in Bathinda or Karnal.

A video-first strategy anchored the account. Reels became the primary vehicle, with consistent output that prioritised emotional storytelling over promotional messaging ‚Äî student success stories, family reactions, honest process walkthroughs.

After a mid-journey account suspension, the rebuild phase became an opportunity to apply everything learned: sharper hooks, tighter CTAs, and a content calendar that balanced viral potential with long-term trust building.
